About Me

I am an early-career computer scientist specializing in data science, automation, and cloud-based data engineering. I am currently working in an independent position to deliver a scalable, HIPAA-compliant ETL pipeline for integrating multi-sourced and multi-formatted EMR data into a standardized schema using a script-driven command pattern enabled via polymorphic deserialization.

My skills in ETL, Data Analytics, and Workflow Automation were first developed through brute-force methods made necessary by the realities of laboratory operations. These skills developed via intuition are now backed by formal education, professional experience, and an ever-growing full-stack approach.

In addition to automating batching, correcting years' worth of historical test data, and automating database transactions from LIMS, etc... I can build dashboards that result in actionable insights, whether they report trended quality/business data or use data mining to identify trends in a sample cluster. I can build a pipeline for transorming or utilizing your data and a front end for your technicians and analysts to interact with it.

While the titles at my previous roles might not reflect it. I found as someone hired for laboratory support the labs more often needed support accessing, understanding, or utilizing their data than they needed getting the actual tests done (when a test takes two hours, data entry takes 30 mins, and seeing the data in the database takes a few more hours this is an issue). My diverse background working in labs across a variety of industries with an even larger variety of responsibilities fosters an intuitive understanding of the data in your lab's workflows. I have the skills necessary to modernize and streamline your lab processes, and I am looking to do just that.
